# Episodic Memory

**Summary**: Short-time-slice, experience-near, predominantly image-based records of goal-processing junctures. In the SMS framework, episodic memories are forgotten rapidly unless they integrate with the [autobiographical-knowledge-base](/autobiographical-knowledge-base). They are also the route to [autonoetic-consciousness](/autonoetic-consciousness).

**Sources**: Conway_2005.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## Ten characteristics

Conway lists ten properties of episodic memories (source: Conway_2005.pdf, Table 3):

1. Summary records of sensory-perceptual-conceptual-affective processing derived from working memory.
2. Patterns of activation and inhibition retained over long periods.
3. Predominantly represented as visual images.
4. Represent short time slices, determined by changes in goal processing.
5. Represented roughly in their order of occurrence.
6. Retained durably only if they become linked to conceptual autobiographical knowledge. Otherwise rapidly forgotten.
7. Their main function is short-term goal-tracking.
8. Recollectively experienced when accessed (the feeling of "remembering").
9. When part of autobiographical memory construction, they provide specificity.
10. Neuroanatomically may be in brain regions separate from conceptual autobiographical networks.

## Time slice and goal change

A key empirical finding: changes in activity mark event boundaries. Recent episodic memories start with actions that indicate goal change (departing home, meeting someone) rather than with thoughts or feelings. This supports the SMS proposal that episodic memories form at goal-processing junctures, not arbitrarily.

In a one-week follow-up, almost none of the episodic detail was retained. What participants recalled was mainly what they had written about the experience a week earlier. This is consistent with rapid forgetting unless integration occurs.

## Two memory systems

Conway proposes that episodic memory is phylogenetically older than the conceptual-autobiographical system. It is a correspondence-biased, image-based, goal-tracking system that many animals likely share. The conceptual-autobiographical system, by contrast, is coherence-biased and may be uniquely human or near-uniquely. The hippocampus likely bridges them.

This has the testable prediction that organisms with only the older system cannot engage in long-term planning. Patients with anterograde amnesia, who have the older system disrupted, are severely impaired at executing long-term goals.

## Engineering analogue

Episodic buffers in AI agents (the typical short-term memory in chat systems) implement the older system. [clive-wearing](/clive-wearing) shows that this is enough to operate moment to moment but not to construct identity. [youssef-2026-psychology-and-ai-memory](/youssef-2026-psychology-and-ai-memory) argues that without the conceptual-autobiographical layer above, agents cannot construct a coherent self over time.
