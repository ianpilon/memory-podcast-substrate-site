# Self as Process (Selflets)

**Summary**: Levin's process-philosophy framing of the Self. The continuous Self is the deeper reality; snapshot-Selves (Selflets) are low-dimensional projections of that flow. Memories are messages between Selflets across time.

**Sources**: Self-Improvising Memory.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## The Pessoa paradox

Levin opens the paper with a Fernando Pessoa quote: to feel today what one felt yesterday is not to feel; it is to remember today what one felt yesterday but live as today's living corpse of what yesterday was lived and lost. The paradox: to be is to change, but to change is to cease being what one was (source: Self-Improvising Memory.pdf).

The Western philosophical resolution is Process Philosophy (Whitehead and others). The Eastern one is Buddhist no-Self or Indic Self-as-non-dual-with-world. Both reject Self-as-thing in favour of Self-as-process.

## Selflets

In Levin's framing, what we experience as a continuous Self is a temporally extended pattern. Each "now" is a Selflet, a few hundred milliseconds thick. The continuous stream of cognition is a series of Selflets, like the slices in the bread-loaf model of Special Relativity (Self-Improvising Memory.pdf, Figure 1).

Memories are then messages between Selflets across time. Each engram is a stigmergic note left in the body by a past version of "us." This re-cast turns memory into communication: the same operation we use horizontally to communicate with other people, used vertically to communicate with our own past and future Selves.

## Why this matters

Three consequences:

- **Memories must be interpreted, not just received.** Just as we interpret others' utterances, we interpret our own past engrams. The recipient (the future Selflet) has allegiance to its own current context, not to the sender's intent.
- **No fact-of-the-matter about "what really happened."** What did you experience? The answer is critically tied to the timescale on which the answer is given. In the short term, two people who saw the same event will largely agree. On a long timescale, the meaning each carries diverges.
- **The Self is what improvises.** A Self that cannot improvise its own past content is not really a Self in Levin's sense. It is a static record. Biological Selves do not commit to a fixed set of past meanings; their freedom lies in forward-looking sense-making.

## Connection to consciousness

Levin proposes, extending Solms, that **consciousness may be palpated uncertainty about your own memories and internal states**. The standard view is palpated uncertainty about the outside world. The extension is that the inner world is equally uncertain and the Self is the act of holding it together.

This is the experiential side of [memories-as-agents](/memories-as-agents) and the experiential bridge to [autonoetic-consciousness](/autonoetic-consciousness) in Conway.
