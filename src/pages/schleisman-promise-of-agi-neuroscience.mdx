# Schleisman: The Promise of AGI and Neuroscience

**Summary**: Schleisman article arguing that current LLM architectures are not brain-like, and that AGI progress depends on developing meso-level theories that bridge micro-scale neuron data and macro-scale cognitive models. Cautions against the historical pattern of mistaking the cutting-edge technology metaphor for actual brain function.

**Sources**: The promise-of-agi-neuroscience.pdf (Schleisman article)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## What the article argues

The brain has been metaphorically described, in order, as a hydraulic system (Descartes, 17th c), an electrical system (Galvani, 18th c), telegraph wires (Helmholtz, 19th c), and a computer (McCulloch and Pitts, 20th c). If history is any guide, the current "brain works like a neural net" metaphor is also likely to be wrong (source: The promise-of-agi-neuroscience.pdf).

The supposition underlying most AI research is the computational theory of mind (CTM): mental states are realized through computation. Even if true, it does not follow that massive connectionist nets like LLMs are brain-like.

## Five reasons LLMs are not brain-like

1. **Neurons are not dumb integrators**. Single human neurons can solve the XOR problem, indicating much richer computation per cell than the textbook model neuron.
2. **Synaptic plasticity is hardware-level**. New synaptic processes physically grow and wither; synapses enlarge with learning. Neural nets only change weights.
3. **Cellular diversity**. Visual cortex V1 alone has 50 distinct interneuron types. Neural nets use homogeneous units.
4. **Glial cells are unaccounted for**. They outnumber neurons 3:1 and actively participate in learning and synaptic plasticity.
5. **Whole-brain integration**. Subcortical systems support different complex cognitions in parallel with cortex. AI overemphasizes cortex because its 6-layer organization maps cleanly to neural nets.

## The meso-level gap

Neuroscience has detailed micro-scale knowledge (single neurons, signaling). Cognitive neuroscience has macro-scale models (regional networks, behaviour correspondences). What is missing is meso-level descriptions of how particular cognitive functions are realized. Without meso-theories of language, complex reasoning, and explicit memory, human-like AGI remains far off.

The Human Connectome Project is still incomplete. The only organism with a fully mapped connectome is C. elegans, with 302 neurons. The Human Brain Project has yielded disappointing results.

## Disciplines needed

The article argues that AGI development needs more than engineering and computer science. Cognitive psychology, cognitive neuroscience, psychometrics, and philosophy each bring decades of relevant work. Philosophy in particular is needed for the ethics that will follow.
