# Retrieval Models: Generative vs Direct

**Summary**: The two routes by which autobiographical memories are constructed. Generative retrieval is iterative cue-elaboration that descends the hierarchy; direct retrieval is the rapid Proustian "madeleine" experience where a specific cue immediately matches stored knowledge.

**Sources**: Conway_2005.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## Generative retrieval

Most cued autobiographical recall is generative. The process (source: Conway_2005.pdf):

1. A cue (e.g., a word like "Bicycle") activates part of the [autobiographical-knowledge-base](/autobiographical-knowledge-base).
2. The cue is elaborated using current goals and accessible self-images. In over 60% of trials, this initial elaboration ties the cue to current life ("I can see my bike at home in the garage").
3. The iterative search descends the hierarchy (lifetime period → general event → specific episodes).
4. Over multiple elaboration cycles (usually under five), a vivid episodic image arrives and search terminates.

Average retrieval time for cued autobiographical memory is 5-7 seconds. By contrast, factual lookup (autobiographical or otherwise) averages around 1.2 seconds. The extra time reflects the construction process.

## Direct retrieval

The classic example is Proust's madeleine: a specific cue (the taste of cake dipped in tea) opens "a known period of life" without iterative search. Conway argues this happens for highly specific cues that closely match stored content, especially in:

- PTSD intrusions: very specific cues (a beard like the assailant's) trigger vivid flashbacks.
- Music and sensory cues: smells in particular have strong direct-retrieval power (Chu and Downes, 2002).
- After amnesia recovery: returning memories often come in waves, each triggered directly.

In the laboratory, about 44% of memories arrived at the 2-second probe deadline, before generative search could have completed. These are direct retrievals.

## The role of retrieval models

A retrieval model is a control process of the working self that specifies what classes of knowledge must combine for a mental representation to be a memory. Minimally it requires:

- Recollective experience ([autonoetic-consciousness](/autonoetic-consciousness)).
- Inward turning of attention.
- Specific types of knowledge entering consciousness.

Retrieval models develop in childhood under socialization and cultural influence. Infants likely have memories that are episodic-like but lack retrieval models. This is one account of [autonoetic-consciousness](/autonoetic-consciousness) development and the late emergence of childhood-amnesia-resistant memory.

## Engineering analogue

Most AI agent retrieval today is direct: an embedding match returns the nearest item. Conway's framework suggests the better model is generative: elaborate the cue using current goals (the [working-self](/working-self)) before searching. This is the implicit architecture in agent frameworks that route through a planner before tool use.
