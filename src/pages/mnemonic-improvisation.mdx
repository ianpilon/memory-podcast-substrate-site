# Mnemonic Improvisation

**Summary**: Levin's term for the dynamic ability to rewrite and remap information (memories) onto new media and new contexts. The active sense-making counterpart of storage. Levin proposes it as the source of morphogenetic robustness and, eventually, conventional intelligence.

**Sources**: Self-Improvising Memory.pdf, self-improvising-memories.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## The claim

Biological agents are "essentially epistemically vulnerable": their parts are subject to mutation, aging, cancer, and hacking by other biota. A static memory architecture would be useless in such a world. What evolved instead is the capacity to take whatever traces remain from a past Self and creatively re-interpret them to serve a current, possibly very different, Self (source: Self-Improvising Memory.pdf).

Levin provisionally names this **mnemonic improvisation**. The commitment is to keeping salience invariant across radical change, not to keeping details intact.

## Caterpillar to butterfly

The load-bearing example. A caterpillar trained to associate red with food largely dissolves its brain during metamorphosis. The butterfly that emerges has a different body, lives in a different world (3D vs 2D), and eats different food (nectar vs leaves). Yet some learned associations survive.

The puzzle is not where the memory is "stored" during metamorphosis. The deeper puzzle is how the engram can be **interpreted and used adaptively by a completely different body** in the context of which the original details would be useless. The new being does not bring specific memories. It brings the deep lessons in a form deployable in the new embodiment.

This is the central case for mnemonic improvisation: the memory is not the data, it is the active rebuild process.

## Generalization across scales

Levin argues the same architecture appears at every scale:

- **Cellular**: regulating networks process metabolic and developmental memories.
- **Tissue**: bioelectric pattern memories in regeneration (planarian fragments rebuilding whole bodies).
- **Organism**: behavioural and episodic memories.
- **Swarm**: ant colonies as "liquid brains" without stable connectivity.
- **Lineage**: genomic DNA as the engram of the evolutionary-scale individual; gene expression is its interpretation.

In each case, the medium changes but the principle is the same: compress, then creatively re-inflate.

## Why this matters

Mnemonic improvisation is Levin's biology-level answer to the same question Conway answered at the psychology level: how does an agent persist across change? Conway: through goal-driven reconstruction biased toward [coherence-vs-correspondence](/coherence-vs-correspondence). Levin: through reinterpretation that preserves salience over detail.

The two frameworks converge on [memory-as-navigation-thesis](/memory-as-navigation-thesis): memory is not storage, it is the active maintenance of an agent across time.
