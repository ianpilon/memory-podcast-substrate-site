# Levin 2024: Blog Supplement and Comment Thread

**Summary**: Michael Levin's blog post about the Self-Improvising Memory paper, plus a 40-response comment thread. The thread surfaces useful extensions: a Lisa Feldman Barrett bridge to constructed emotion, an economics analogy (prices as signals), Jung's Philemon as proto-experience of agential sub-components, and a long RL/RNN exchange with David Bloomin.

**Sources**: self-improvising-memories.pdf (Forms of life, forms of mind blog, 16 June 2024)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## What the supplement adds

The post itself reprints quotes from the paper and adds Levin's framing on inner-voice phenomena (e.g., the case of a woman whose hallucinated voice correctly diagnosed a brain tumour, ending with "We are pleased to have helped you. Goodbye"). Levin uses this to argue that communication with one's agential sub-components is the key to a "definitive medical roadmap" and is being actively researched (source: self-improvising-memories.pdf).

A crucial author clarification from the comments: "I suspect the synaptic machinery is mostly for interpretation of the memory, not storage. There is no single substrate for memory. Biology treats all the parts, including bioelectric, biomechanical, and all biochemical events, as a kind of reservoir." This is more direct than the paper itself and sharpens [no-single-substrate](/no-single-substrate).

## Extensions from the comment thread

- **Benjamin L** offers an economics analogy: prices are "signals wrapped up in incentives." Like Levin's bowtie engrams, prices compress huge amounts of personal information into a low-dimensional medium that strangers can re-inflate and act on. The supply-and-demand response loop matches the active-data view in [memories-as-agents](/memories-as-agents).
- **Benjamin L** also connects the paper to Lisa Feldman Barrett's [constructed-emotion-barrett](/constructed-emotion-barrett): concepts are competing neural patterns that win by best matching incoming data for allostasis. Levin had not previously known the connection and asked for references.
- **Mirka Misáková** raises Jung's Philemon and Internal Family Systems (IFS) therapy as historical glimpses of communicating with agential sub-components. Levin: "We have to move toward tools for systems that are not verbal."
- **David Bloomin** (an RL researcher) maps the framework onto recurrent neural networks (RNNs):
  - F_encoder(observation) → latent
  - F_rnn(latent, last_engram) → next_engram
  - F_action(engram) → action

  Levin's response: not sure yet whether RNNs capture the creative-interpretation right side of the [bowtie-architecture](/bowtie-architecture). The crux is whether an RNN can "support radical changes in the agent after learning."

## Why this matters

The blog supplement is where Levin commits to "no single substrate" more clearly than in the paper, and where the bridges to AI agent memory ([ai-memory-as-identity-construction](/ai-memory-as-identity-construction)) and to affective psychology ([constructed-emotion-barrett](/constructed-emotion-barrett)) first appear publicly. The RNN exchange is also the explicit acknowledgement that current ML memory designs do not yet implement [memories-as-agents](/memories-as-agents).
