# Autonoetic Consciousness

**Summary**: Tulving's term for the felt sense of "remembering," distinct from "knowing." It is the experiential signal that an activated mental representation is a memory rather than a fantasy, daydream, or fact. Conway treats it as the conscious side of the [self-memory-system](/self-memory-system)'s integration process.

**Sources**: Conway_2005.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## What it is

Autonoetic consciousness is "mental time travel" in Tulving's phrase: the felt re-experiencing of the past as something one lived through. This is distinguished from noetic consciousness (the feeling of familiarity that comes with semantic knowledge) and anoetic states (no conscious feeling about the source).

The function is to **signal** that one is in fact remembering. It does not entail accurate re-experiencing. As Conway notes, what matters is the feeling of remembering, not the literal fidelity of the content (source: Conway_2005.pdf).

## Why it exists

In SMS terms, autonoetic consciousness is what happens when a stable activation pattern in the [autobiographical-knowledge-base](/autobiographical-knowledge-base) interlocks with [episodic-memory](/episodic-memory) under the supervision of the [working-self](/working-self). The integration is the memory; the feeling is the signal that the integration occurred.

This explains why episodic memories are recollectively experienced (Table 3, item VIII in [episodic-memory](/episodic-memory)) while conceptual autobiographical knowledge can be known without the same felt re-experience.

## When it goes wrong

Conway and colleagues studied patient AKP, who suffered chronic and intense déjà vécu (the feeling of having lived the present moment before). On formal recognition memory tests, AKP made twice the false-positive rate of controls. Strikingly, AKP labelled most of those false positives as recollective (he was sure he was remembering) and could justify them with plausible-sounding details ("Polka is Polish for female").

The lesion appeared to be in frontal control circuits, not in temporal lobe memory storage. The recollective-experience circuit ran unchecked. AKP described the studio, the clothes, and the questions of an interviewer he had never met before, confidently confabulating present perception as remembered past.

This shows that autonoetic consciousness is a **separable signal** that can fire without an actual memory underneath. See [ptsd-and-memory-distortion](/ptsd-and-memory-distortion) for related cases.

## Why it matters

Autonoetic consciousness is the inside-view counterpart to [memories-as-agents](/memories-as-agents). Levin argues that engrams are agential prompts; Conway argues that the feeling of remembering is the working self's signal that an integration has occurred. Both treat the experience of memory as constructed, not retrieved.

This is also where the wiki connects to consciousness research more broadly. Levin's hypothesis in [levin-2024-blog-supplement](/levin-2024-blog-supplement) ("consciousness is palpated uncertainty about your own memories and internal states") extends Solms's "palpated uncertainty about the outside world" by adding internal uncertainty. Autonoetic consciousness is one expression of that.
