# Working Self

**Summary**: The active, goal-hierarchy component of the [self-memory-system](/self-memory-system). It modulates memory accessibility and inaccessibility in service of current goals and self-coherence. Without it, retrieval would be undirected and identity-incoherent.

**Sources**: Conway_2005.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## What the working self is

A highly complex hierarchy of interlocking positive and negative feedback loops, with goals represented at different levels of specificity. Its function is to reduce discrepancies between desired and current states, and in doing so, regulate behaviour, cognition, and affect (source: Conway_2005.pdf).

It has two parts:

1. **Goal hierarchy proper**: a temporally specified, currently active set of intentions and sub-goals.
2. **Conceptual self**: non-temporal self-knowledge structures such as personal scripts, self-images, self-guides, possible selves, self-with-other units, attitudes, values, and beliefs. These are not memories of specific events but the abstracted patterns that organize them.

## What it does to memory

The working self modulates both:

- **Encoding**: which experiences are deeply encoded depends on their relevance to currently active goals.
- **Retrieval**: control processes determine which knowledge can enter consciousness and become a memory. Memories that threaten coherence can be inhibited.

This is the source of motivated forgetting, of confabulation under frontal damage, and of memory distortion in PTSD. See [ptsd-and-memory-distortion](/ptsd-and-memory-distortion) for clinical cases where the working self produces false but coherence-protective memories.

## The conservatism principle

Conway and colleagues argue that at the heart of the working self is a principle of conservatism: resist goal change. Goal change is cognitively and affectively costly because every change has consequences for many other goals. So the working self will distort memory rather than allow disruptive goal-revision. Greenwald (1980) called this the "totalitarian ego."

The cost: memory distortion. The benefit: a stable, operative self that can pursue long-term goals across changing circumstances.
